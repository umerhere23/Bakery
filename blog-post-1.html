The Art of Sourdough: Our 100-Year-Old Starter

By Sofia Rodriguez 5 min read

At Sweet Delights Bakery, our sourdough bread holds a special place in our hearts and in our ovens. What makes it truly unique is our heritage sourdough starter that's been lovingly maintained for over 100 years.

The History of Our Starter

Our starter was originally created in 1920 by Maria's grandmother, Isabella, in a small village in northern Spain. When Isabella immigrated to England in 1955, she brought the starter with her in a small clay pot, and it's been the foundation of our sourdough ever since.

"A good sourdough starter is like a family member - it needs regular care and attention, but it repays you with incredible flavor and texture." - Sofia Rodriguez, Head Baker

Maintaining the Starter

We feed our starter twice daily with equal parts organic whole wheat flour and filtered water. The consistency should resemble thick pancake batter. Here's our basic maintenance routine:

  • Morning: Remove half the starter, then feed with 100g flour and 100g water
  • Evening: Stir well and leave at room temperature (about 20°C)
  • Weekly: Deep clean the container and document the starter's activity

Our active sourdough starter after feeding

Baking Day Process

Our sourdough baking process takes 36 hours from start to finish:

  1. Levain creation: 8 hours before mixing, we prepare the levain
  2. Autolyse: Mix flour and water, let rest for 1 hour
  3. Bulk fermentation: 4 hours with 4 sets of stretch and folds
  4. Shaping: Pre-shape, rest 30 minutes, then final shape
  5. Cold proof: 12-18 hours in the refrigerator
  6. Baking: 30 minutes covered at 250°C, then 20 minutes uncovered at 230°C

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Even with an established starter, challenges can arise:

Issue Possible Cause Solution
Dense crumb Underproofed dough Extend bulk fermentation time
Too sour Overfermented Reduce fermentation time or temperature
Flat loaf Weak gluten development Increase kneading/stretch and folds

Our Basic Sourdough Recipe

  • 500g strong white bread flour
  • 100g whole wheat flour
  • 400g water (80% hydration)
  • 100g active sourdough starter
  • 10g sea salt

Yield: 1 large loaf or 2 small loaves
